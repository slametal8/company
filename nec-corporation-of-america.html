NEC Corporation of America: The Complete Profile

NEC's global logo represents its commitment to technological innovation across all markets

NEC Corporation of America (NECAM) stands as the cornerstone of Japanese technology giant NEC's operations in North America, serving as both a technology innovator and trusted solutions provider across multiple industries. With its headquarters in Irving, Texas, and regional offices strategically located throughout the United States, NECAM has grown from its modest beginnings as a sales outpost to become a comprehensive technology powerhouse addressing the most pressing challenges in IT infrastructure, telecommunications, public safety, and enterprise solutions.

Quick Facts

  • Founded: 1963 (as Nippon Electric New York)
  • Current Headquarters: Irving, Texas
  • Parent Company: NEC Corporation (Tokyo, Japan)
  • Employees: Approximately 2,100 across North America
  • Annual Revenue: $1.8 billion (2022 estimate)
  • Key Markets: Telecommunications, Public Safety, Enterprise IT, Biometrics

Corporate Evolution and Historical Milestones

The story of NEC Corporation of America reflects both the globalization of Japanese technology firms and the evolution of the U.S. technology landscape over the past six decades. What began as a modest sales operation has transformed into a multifaceted technology leader through strategic acquisitions, organic growth, and continuous innovation.

1963

Establishment of Nippon Electric New York

NEC's first U.S. presence began with the incorporation of Nippon Electric New York, primarily focused on selling telecommunications equipment to American businesses and government agencies. The company initially specialized in microwave communications systems and early digital switching equipment.

1971

Expansion into Computer Systems

NEC America began distributing the company's mainframe computer systems in the U.S. market, marking its entry into the growing data processing industry. These systems were particularly popular with research institutions and financial services firms.

1981

Creation of NEC Home Electronics (USA)

Recognizing the growing consumer electronics market, NEC established a dedicated division for home electronics products. The division would later become famous for its high-quality computer monitors and professional displays.

1986

HNSX Supercomputers Joint Venture

NEC formed HNSX Supercomputers in partnership with Honeywell to compete in the lucrative U.S. supercomputing market. This venture marked NEC's first major attempt to penetrate the high-performance computing sector in America.

1997

Supercomputer Dumping Controversy

The U.S. International Trade Commission found HNSX and Fujitsu guilty of dumping supercomputers below cost to win a contract with the National Center for Atmospheric Research. This ruling significantly impacted NEC's supercomputing strategy in America.

2006

Formation of NEC Corporation of America

NEC consolidated its three major U.S. operations (NEC America, NEC Solutions America, and NEC USA) into a single entity to streamline operations and better serve the North American market.

2018

Expansion into AI and Biometrics

NECAM made significant investments in artificial intelligence and biometric identification technologies, particularly facial recognition systems that would become widely adopted by law enforcement agencies.

2022

5G Network Solutions Launch

The company introduced a comprehensive suite of 5G networking solutions for telecommunications providers, positioning itself as a key player in next-generation wireless infrastructure.
