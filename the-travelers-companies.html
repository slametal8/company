The Travelers Companies: A Legacy of Protection

Travelers' headquarters at 385 Washington Street in St. Paul, Minnesota

The Travelers Companies, Inc. stands as one of the most respected and enduring names in the American insurance industry. As the second-largest writer of U.S. commercial property casualty insurance and sixth-largest writer of U.S. personal insurance through independent agents, Travelers has built a reputation for financial strength, innovative products, and exceptional claims service over its nearly 170-year history.

Key Facts at a Glance

  • Founded: 1853 (as St. Paul Fire and Marine Insurance Company)
  • Headquarters: New York, NY (operational HQ in Hartford, CT)
  • Incorporated: Minnesota
  • 2022 Revenue: $36.87 billion
  • 2022 Net Income: $2.83 billion
  • Employees: Approximately 30,000
  • Dow Jones Component: Since June 2009
  • Fortune 500 Rank: #98 (2021)

Corporate History and Evolution

The Travelers Companies traces its roots back to 1853 when the St. Paul Fire and Marine Insurance Company was founded in Minnesota Territory. The company's history reflects the growth of the American insurance industry and the nation's economic development.

1853

Founding of St. Paul Fire and Marine

Established to provide fire insurance to Minnesota's growing frontier communities, the company survived the Panic of 1857 and expanded throughout the Midwest.

1864

First Dividend Paid

The company demonstrated early financial stability by paying its first dividend to shareholders just 11 years after founding.

1953

Centennial Celebration

Marking 100 years in business, the company had grown to become a respected national insurer with assets exceeding $100 million.

1984

St. Paul Companies Formed

The holding company structure was created to better manage diversified insurance operations.

1997

Acquisition of USF&G

The $3.9 billion purchase of USF&G Corporation significantly expanded St. Paul's market presence.

2004

Merger with Travelers Property Casualty

The $16 billion merger created The St. Paul Travelers Companies, combining two industry leaders.

2007

Rebranding as The Travelers Companies

The company adopted the better-known Travelers name while maintaining St. Paul's Minnesota incorporation.

2009

Added to Dow Jones Industrial Average

Travelers joined the prestigious Dow 30 index, reflecting its importance in the financial sector.

Business Segments and Operations

Travelers operates through three primary business segments, each serving distinct market needs while sharing common underwriting discipline and risk management practices.

Business Insurance

Travelers' largest segment provides property and casualty products for mid-sized and large businesses:

  • Workers' Compensation: Market leader with innovative return-to-work programs
  • Commercial Auto: Coverage for business vehicle fleets
  • Commercial Property: Protection for buildings and business personal property
  • General Liability: Protection against third-party claims
  • Professional Liability: Coverage for errors and omissions

2022 Segment Revenue: $19.3 billion

Bond & Specialty Insurance

This segment focuses on surety bonds and specialty liability coverages:

  • Surety Bonds: For construction projects and court proceedings
  • Management Liability: D&O, E&O, and fiduciary coverages
  • Professional Indemnity: For architects, engineers, and other professionals
  • Cyber Insurance: Growing product line addressing digital risks

2022 Segment Revenue: $3.5 billion

Personal Insurance

Serving individuals through independent agents with:

  • Homeowners Insurance: Protection for dwellings and personal property
  • Automobile Insurance: Personal auto coverage with innovative telematics
  • Umbrella Liability: Additional liability protection
  • Recreational Products: Coverage for boats, motorcycles, etc.

2022 Segment Revenue: $13.9 billion

Travelers is known for its stable, experienced leadership team and strong corporate governance practices.

Chairman and Chief Executive Officer

Daniel S. Frey

Executive Vice President & Chief Financial Officer

Conclusion

From its humble beginnings in 1853 Minnesota to its current position as a Dow Jones Industrial Average component, The Travelers Companies has demonstrated remarkable resilience and adaptability.
